I am currently a third-year master's student in the School of Urban Design at Wuhan University, majoring in Architecture. My research investigates the intersection of the built environment and human well-being through computational social science. I leverage novel data sources, such as social media, to quantify the impact of urban design on public mental health, aiming to develop frameworks for more human-centric urban policy.

Urban studies

Geospatial analysis, scaling law analysis, spatial heterogeneity analysis

Mental health

Public sentiment measurement, public depression perception

Architecture

Environmental psychology, architectural design

Data science

Machine learning, deep learning, large language model deployment

My research journey began in 2023. Under the guidance of Prof. Zhang Xia and Prof. Tang Luliang, my research explores how public data can reveal public perceptions of the urban built environment.

My first project, which was recently published in Sustainable Cities and Society, developed an analytical framework for assessing public preferences in commercial spaces. This study leveraged data from Dazhong Dianping and applied a methodological combination of BERT (for sentiment analysis), Random Forest, and SHAP.

Building on this, my current research investigates the critical association between mental health (specifically depressive states) and perceived built environments. Using Weibo dataset, we provide the first large-scale quantification of this fine-grained association in China. We recently resubmitted the manuscript after the first round of revisions in Nature Cities... and are keeping our fingers crossed :)

First author, Sustainable Cities and Society, 119, 106120. (IF: 12.0, JCR Q1)

CBEES: A fine-grained commercial built environment perception and evaluation based on social media

● National Natural Science Foundation of China (NSFC) General Program Proposal

Jan 2025 - Mar 2025.

Project Participant, “Research on Restorative Effect Mechanisms and Quantitative Optimization Design of Urban Open Spaces Based on Multimodal Data Fusion”.


Designed a technical workflow for dynamic crowd activity analysis: proposed using YOLOv8 for pedestrian detection, combined Faster R-CNN and DeepSORT algorithms for activity recognition, and applied BERT models to extract activity duration from social media text.

Built computational models using Graph Neural Networks (GNN) to simulate structural relationships between built environment factors and individual healing effects.

Proposed the "Experiments and Validation" plan: utilized Virtual Reality (VR) to create pre/post-optimization scenarios and employed wearable devices to collect physiological data (e.g., HRV, EDA) for objective validation.

This section primarily showcases projects from my undergraduate studies. Although my focus shifted to quantitative research during graduate school, my admiration for architectural design have remained. For instance, in my first year of graduate study, I collaborated with a colleague on the Young Architects Competitions (YAC), receiving an Honorable Mention for our art gallery design in Weihai. This was immensely encouraging, as the jury included experts from Pritzker Prize-winning studios, such as David Chipperfield Architects.

This section primarily showcases projects from my undergraduate studies. Although my focus shifted to quantitative research during graduate school, my admiration for architectural design have remained. For instance, in my first year of graduate study, I collaborated with a colleague on the Young Architects Competitions (YAC), receiving an Honorable Mention for our art gallery design in Weihai. This was immensely encouraging, as the jury included experts from Pritzker Prize-winning studios, such as David Chipperfield Architects.

The 6th "Zhonglian Cup" competition (Excellence Award) during my undergraduate years was another pivotal point. In this large-scale national competition, I independently designed a community center. I conducted questionnaire-based research to deeply understand residents' needs, using the data to inform the allocation and design of functional spaces. Interestingly, this experience sparked my interest in data and quantitative research, directly guiding my subsequent academic direction in graduate school.

I started photography in 2018. It has always been a personal hobby for me, rather than commercial pursuit. I aim for a cinematic tone, focusing on atmosphere, light, shadow, and emotion, trying to build an idealized state of mind in my photos. For portraits, I prefer natural light. I enjoy capturing the interaction between people and the earth, the environment, and the place itself—it brings me peace.

I started photography in 2018. It has always been a personal hobby for me, rather than commercial pursuit. I aim for a cinematic tone, focusing on atmosphere, light, shadow, and emotion, trying to build an idealized state of mind in my photos. For portraits, I prefer natural light. I enjoy capturing the interaction between people and the earth, the environment, and the place itself—it brings me peace.

This section also includes some of my render works, all created before 2022 (the "Pre-AI" era). I am drawn to surrealism and the sense of the sublime in architectural spaces. I often imagine myself in vast, empty places with a heavy, booming sound overhead, which is likely related to my admiration for Louis Kahn's architecture. Since AI image generation became commonplace, I've found myself stepping back from this field.
